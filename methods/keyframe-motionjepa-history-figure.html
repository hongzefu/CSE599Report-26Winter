Figure 1. Overall architecture of our keyframe-MotionJEPA memory pipeline. A 48-frame robot trajectory is reduced to a sparse keyframe set by CLIP Δ-distance peak detection (① τ-thresholded); each later keyframe is then sparsified by ② TokenDrop, which keeps only the bounding rectangle of patches that differ significantly from the reference keyframe at t=0. In parallel, every fixed Δ=8 window of frames (③) is encoded by a frozen SigLIP pair (④) and compressed by a MotionJEPA encoder (⑤) into a single motion token. The two streams are merged into a combined history sequence (⑥) aligned to the input timeline, yielding a compact memory of … that conditions the downstream policy (⑦) for action prediction.
